Smart shoes designed for visually impaired individuals feature integrated sensors that enhance their mobility and safety. These sensors, typically ultrasonic or LiDAR-based, continuously scan the surroundings, detecting obstacles and changes in terrain. The data collected is transmitted to a connected device, such as a smartphone or wearable, which then provides auditory or haptic feedback to the user. This feedback informs them about the distance and location of obstacles, helping them navigate confidently and avoid potential hazards. These innovative shoes are a promising assistive technology, offering greater independence and security for people with visual impairments, allowing them to explore their environment more freely.

Solar cars are eco-friendly vehicles powered by electricity generated from solar panels mounted on their surfaces. These photovoltaic cells convert sunlight into electrical energy, which is stored in batteries or directly used to drive the vehicle's electric motor. Solar cars are highly sustainable and produce zero emissions, making them a promising solution for reducing carbon footprints in transportation. While they are primarily used for racing and experimental purposes due to limitations in energy production and storage, ongoing advancements in solar technology and battery efficiency are gradually making them more practical for everyday use. Solar cars represent a clean and renewable energy future for the automotive industry.
